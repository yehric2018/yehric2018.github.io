Welcome!

My name is Eric (20, he/him), an incoming junior at the University of Washington majoring in computer science and minoring in neural computation and engineering. Although I still have two/three years left, I've become a proficient programmer through coursework, research, and other projects.

This summer, I'm working at the Hood-Price Lab in the Institute for Systems Biology writing software to parse genomes for DNA sequences called transposable elements. To keep myself busy outside of classes, I've also been involved with research in UW's Taskar Center for Accessible Technology, working on a React Native app that will help create safe routes to schools, and being a TA for CSE 344, an introductory class on data management.

I created this website not only to polish up my web-dev skills, but also as an expressive outlet for the things I'm thinking about (that's what the blog's for). Social media isn't really the best place to be your true self, so I hope this can be a place on the Internet just for me. I encourage you to do the same. :)
